Pork Katsudon

About the Dish

Katsudon is a Japanese pork cutlet rice bowl made with Tonkatsu, eggs, and sauteed onions simmered in a sweet and savory sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 center-cut boneless pork chops, pounded down to 1 centimeter thick
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • All-purpose flour, for dusting
  • 5 large eggs, beaten, divided
  • 1 cup panko
  • Oil, for frying
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 1 1/4 cups dashi soup stock
  • 4 cups
  • 2 tablespoons mirin

Instructions

  1. Season the pounded pork chops with salt and pepper
  2. Dust with a light, even coating of flour
  3. In one shallow bowl, beat 1 of the eggs. Put the panko into another shallow bowl
  4. Add a thin, even layer of oil to a cast-iron pan or skillet over medium heat
  5. Dip the flour-dusted pork into the egg to coat both sides
  6. Transfer the pork to the panko and press it evenly into the meat to get a good coating
  7. Carefully lay the pork chops in the hot oil and cook for 5 to 6 minutes on one side, until golden brown
  8. Flip and cook the other side for another 5 to 6 minutes, or until browned, crispy
  9. Slice your tonkatsu into pieces
  10. Put the dashi soup stock in a pan and heat on medium heat
  11. Add the soy sauce, mirin, and sugar to the soup and bring to a boil. Remove from the heat
  12. Add 1 serving of tonkatsu pieces (half of 1 pork cutlet) to the pan and simmer on low heat for a few minutes
  13. Beat another one of the eggs in a bowl. Bring the soup to a boil and pour the egg over the tonkatsu
  14. Turn the heat down to low and cover with a lid. Cook until the egg has set and remove it from the heat. The egg should be cooked through
  15. Serve by placing 1 serving of steamed rice in a large rice bowl
